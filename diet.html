Giraffe Diet

Giraffes primarily eat the leaves and twigs of acacia, mimosa, and wild apricot trees
Giraffes may eat up to 75 pounds (34 kilograms) of food per day.
Although giraffes are herbivores and mostly feed on vegetation such as leaves, grass, twigs, fruit and roots; they also eat some other things.
Such as Acacia Trees with Big Thorns, chewing on Old Bones, Prickly Pears, Poisonous Euphorbia Trees.
Giraffes like Denver have a specially adapted tongue and mouth
that allow them to eat thorny branches without getting poked.
They also have thick sticky saliva that coats any thorns consumed to protect their mouth and digestive tract!
Most of animal can not overpower giraffes alone.
