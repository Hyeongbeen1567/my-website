Species

Northern giraffes

The northern giraffe, also known as three-horned giraffe, is a type of giraffe native to North Africa.
They are often mistaken with the Southern Giraffes,
but Northern giraffes can be differentiated by the shape and size of the two distinctive horn-like protuberances known as ossicones on their foreheads

Southern giraffe

Southern giraffes have rounded or blotched spots, some with star-like extensions on a light tan background, running down to the hooves.
They range from South Africa, Angola, Namibia, Botswana, Zambia, Zimbabwe, Mozambique.
Southern giraffe is most common species of giraffe.
Their approximate population is composed of 44,500 to 50,000 individuals.
